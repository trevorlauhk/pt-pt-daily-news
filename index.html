English

Yesterday, Ana was in downtown Lisbon and wanted to go to Praça do Comércio. She didn’t know the city well, so she decided to ask for directions. First, she saw a kind gentleman on the street. "Excuse me, do you know where Praça do Comércio is?" Ana asked. The man smiled and said, "Of course! Go straight ahead on this street." Then, turn right at the second street. The square is right there." Ana thanked him and started walking. But after five minutes, she realized she wasn’t on the right path. Then, she saw a young woman holding a map. "Excuse me, can you help me? I’m lost," Ana said. The woman looked at the map and explained, "Now, you’re on the wrong street." Go back and turn left at the first street. Then, just go straight until you see the square." This time, Ana followed the instructions carefully. Ten minutes later, she arrived at Praça do Comércio. She was very happy and thought, "Next time, I’ll bring a map too!"

English

In a world increasingly dominated by technology and haste, it is easy to forget the immeasurable value that art and culture bring to our lives. Although many consider these areas mere hobbies, the truth is that they are fundamental pillars of society, since they allow us to express emotions, question the status quo, and, above all, connect with each other in deep and meaningful ways. Imagine, for a moment, a city without museums, theaters, or libraries. It would be a cold and soulless place, where people would live only to work and consume, without space for reflection or creativity. However, art is not just a luxury for the fortunate; it is a right for all, provided it is accessible. That is why initiatives that promote culture in underprivileged neighborhoods are so important, so that no one is deprived of this nourishment for the spirit. Moreover, culture plays a crucial role in preserving a people’s identity. Through music, dance, literature, and traditions, we transmit values and stories that shape who we are. Yet, in times of globalization, it is essential that these expressions do not disappear but rather adapt, so they continue to resonate with future generations. Therefore, supporting local artists and participating in cultural events is not just an act of leisure but a commitment to our own humanity. In short, art and culture are not mere decorations of society; they are its essence. May we never lack the sensitivity to value them or the courage to defend them.
