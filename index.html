Yesterday, João went to the City Hall for the first time. He needed to renew his citizen card. When he arrived, he saw a long queue at the entrance. "I’ll wait a bit," he thought. After twenty minutes, it was his turn. The clerk was very friendly. She asked, "How can I help you?" João explained what he needed. She said, "Do you have all the documents?" João showed his old ID card and a photograph. "Everything is correct. In two weeks, you can come pick up your new card." João was happy. Before leaving, he remembered to ask, "Can I also request the voter card here?" On his way home, João stopped at a pastry shop. And tomorrow he will take care of the voter card!

English

Some say that Portugal has become, in recent years, a mosaic of cultures, where each piece, no matter how small, contributes to a vibrant whole. However, multicultural integration is not a path paved only with good intentions. Although the country has taken significant steps in welcoming immigrants, obstacles still persist, such as the language barrier or unequal access to opportunities. It is therefore essential to create public policies that not only facilitate arrival but also ensure that everyone can flourish, regardless of their origin. Since language is the key to communication, many immigrants strive to learn Portuguese, so that they can feel part of the community. However, mastering a new language is not always easy, especially when daily life demands speed and confidence. Consequently, it is crucial to provide more support, such as free classes or mentorships, so that no one is left behind. Furthermore, companies should recognize the value of diversity, by hiring people from different backgrounds, provided they have the necessary skills. Of course, integration does not depend solely on institutions. Each of us can contribute, whether through a simple gesture, like helping a neighbor fill out a form, or a broader attitude, such as combating prejudice. After all, a more inclusive society is not built overnight, but with patience, empathy, and the willingness to learn from one another. May Portugal continue to be an example not only of hospitality but also of mutual respect.
