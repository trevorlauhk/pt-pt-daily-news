When we arrived, there were a lot of people. People were pushing carts and looking at the shelves. I took a small basket because we only needed a few things. First, we went to the fruit section. My mom chose red apples and bananas. Then, we went to the dairy section. I grabbed a carton of milk and a strawberry yogurt. In the cereal aisle, I saw my favorites: Chocapic! "Mom, can we buy some?" English

In recent years, artificial intelligence (AI) has been transforming our daily lives in ways that, just a few decades ago, would have seemed like science fiction. From virtual assistants that help us organize our schedules to algorithms capable of diagnosing diseases with precision, AI offers countless advantages. However, despite all the benefits, some fear that this technology may one day escape our control and pose a threat to humanity. One of the biggest concerns is the impact of AI on the job market. Many worry that as machines become smarter, they may replace jobs that, until now, were exclusive to humans. Therefore, it is essential that governments and companies invest in retraining workers so that they can adapt to the new demands of the market. Nevertheless, we cannot ignore that AI also creates opportunities, since it frees humans from repetitive tasks and allows them to focus on more creative and meaningful activities. Another controversial point is ethics. It is crucial to establish clear rules to ensure that AI is used responsibly. For example, although algorithms can make quick decisions, they do not always understand the moral nuances that only humans master. Thus, human oversight is vital in sensitive areas such as justice or medicine. In short, AI is neither inherently good nor bad. Everything depends on how we use it. If we manage it wisely, it can be a valuable ally for societal progress. But for this to happen, it is necessary that everyone—from programmers to ordinary citizens—reflects on their role in this new technological world.
