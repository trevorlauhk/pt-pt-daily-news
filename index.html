O 25 de Abril: Uma Revolução que Mudou Portugal para Sempre

English

Almost fifty years ago, Portugal awoke to a new dawn. On April 25, 1974, a peaceful revolution put an end to nearly five decades of dictatorship. Although many believed such a profound change would require violence, the military chose carnations instead of bullets. However, the path to democracy was not simple. Since the previous regime controlled every aspect of public life, it was necessary to rebuild institutions. What makes April 25th so special is the fact that it was a revolution made by the people, for the people. Consequently, freedom of expression, the right to vote, and the possibility of dreaming of a different future became realities. Nevertheless, it is important to remember that democracy is not built overnight. Even today, some question whether the ideals of the revolution have been fully achieved. Despite everything, April 25th continues to be celebrated as a milestone of courage and solidarity. So that future generations do not forget its meaning, schools and families pass down the stories of those days. Therefore, when we see the red carnations in the streets or hear the song "Grândola, Vila Morena," we remember that freedom is a right that must be protected every day.
