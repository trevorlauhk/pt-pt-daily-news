Um Jantar Especial

A2 (CIPLE)

English

Last night, Pedro and Ana went out to dinner at a new restaurant in the city center. They were very happy because it was Ana’s birthday. When they arrived, a friendly waiter said, "Good evening! A table for two?" Pedro answered, "Yes, please." Then they sat near the window, and the waiter brought the menus. Ana looked at the menu and said, "Everything looks delicious!" Pedro laughed and replied, "I think I’ll order the steak with fries." Ana thought for a moment and then said, "I’ll order the chicken salad and a vegetable soup." When the waiter returned, Pedro said, "I’d like a steak with fries, please." Ana said, "And I’d like the chicken salad and a vegetable soup." The waiter wrote everything down and asked, "And to drink?" Pedro answered, "A mineral water, please." Ana said, "I want a fresh orange juice." After some time, the waiter brought the food. Everything was very good! At the end, they asked for the bill and left a tip.

O Futuro da Mobilidade Urbana: Será que os Transportes Públicos Podem Salvar as Nossas Cidades?

B1-B2 (DEPLE/DIPLE)

English

Modern cities face a growing challenge: how to ensure that millions of people can move efficiently without traffic turning into daily chaos? Although many still prefer private cars because they offer convenience, the truth is that public transport emerges as the most sustainable solution and, therefore, the most urgent. However, for this change to happen, it is crucial that governments invest not only in infrastructure but also in campaigns that convince citizens to leave their cars at home. In Lisbon, for example, the metro and buses have been improving; however, there are still those who complain about overcrowding and delays. It is undeniable that, despite the flaws, the benefits outweigh the drawbacks. After all, if everyone chose public transport, the reduction in pollution and time wasted in traffic would be significant. Moreover, it is important that more bike lanes and pedestrian zones be created so that people feel safe opting for more eco-friendly modes of transport. Of course, this transition will not be easy. Many argue that public transport is not accessible to everyone, especially in outlying areas. Therefore, integrated planning is essential, one that includes not only major avenues but also more remote neighborhoods. If authorities can prove that this is a viable option, perhaps citizens will begin to see public transport not as a necessary evil but as a smart and responsible choice.
