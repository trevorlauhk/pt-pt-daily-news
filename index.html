Before the party, I bought a nice gift: a book she really wanted. I arrived at the party at eight in the evening. Rita’s house looked beautiful, full of balloons and colorful lights. All the guests were chatting and laughing. First, we sang happy birthday, and she blew out the candles. I danced with Pedro, a friend of Rita’s. When she saw my book, she shouted, "I loved it! English

For years, Lisbon and Porto have been facing a housing crisis that seems to have no end. Despite soaring property prices, many buildings remain empty, while entire families struggle to find a roof over their heads. It is unacceptable that in a city like Lisbon, there are luxury apartments left vacant while thousands of people cannot afford decent rent. However, the problem is not just about real estate speculation. Since tourism has grown uncontrollably, many landlords prefer to rent their spaces to tourists in order to make quick profits, rather than making them available to local residents. Yet, we cannot blame only foreign investors. The government, although it has implemented some measures, such as the affordable rental program, has still not managed to solve the root of the problem. It is urgent that more effective policies be created, provided that they do not benefit only large developers. Consequently, many young people and middle-class families continue to live in precarious conditions or emigrate in search of a better life. If we do not act now, the future of these cities will be even bleaker. There needs to be a change in mentality, where housing is seen as a right, not a business. Otherwise, Lisbon and Porto risk losing their identity, turning into theme parks for tourists while their inhabitants are pushed to the outskirts. How much longer will we tolerate this injustice?
